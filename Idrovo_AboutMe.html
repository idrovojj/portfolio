About Me

Juan Jose Idrovo Macias

Objectives, Interests, and Strengths

Everyone has been asked the question, "What do you want to be when you grow up?". Although some people answer the question instantly, it has haunted me for years. How are we able to decide our desired profession if we have not experienced its advantages and disadvantages yet? Although guest speakers from a given profession provide information, it is not comparable to first-hand experience. My solution to this predicament is to branch out and enroll in different types of high school courses in order to discover my strengths and weaknesses to a given field. After three years of high school, I have narrowed down my interests to statistics, chemistry, and video production. However, I am currently experimenting and planning to experiment with more subjects such as physics, calculus, and computer engineering.

Not only have the courses revealed my interests, but they have additionally demonstrated and reinforced my abilities. For example, my AP Statistics course involved taking samples, analyzing them, and drawing conclusions about the population. My communication skills improved because I had to ensure information was conveyed correctly between the people being sampled and their results, as well as the statistics to the client who requested the analysis. Furthermore, I am very methodical in my studies, which has provided an advantage throughout my Honors Chemistry course. Nevertheless, the course still bolstered my methodicalness through experimentation, calculations, and conclusions. In fact, this ability allowed me to enroll in AP Chemistry the following year, which contains more heavy calculations and rigorous analysis. Finally, my Advanced Video Production course required immense coordination between screenwriters, actors, cinematographers, and film editors. Through the process of overcoming all of the coordination, I gained leadership. Over the succeeding year, I applied to Video Production 3, which once again tested my leadership skills. Since the class required longer films, the production timeline increased, causing our members to be dispirited. By encouraging my peers, we were able to persevere and complete the project.

Although I am uncertain of what profession I want to specialize into, I am aware of my strengths and their application to different fields of studies. I can communicate information clearly and concisely, execute my work with precision methodically, in addition to lead and coordinate groups to a common goal. As I continue to live out my high school career, I will keep experimenting with different classes to discover and develop more strengths. My objective is to illuminate the dark path of uncertainty until I reach the final decision of my career at the end. I appreciate your time, and if you would like to learn more about my experience, or projects, feel free to browse the rest of my website.
